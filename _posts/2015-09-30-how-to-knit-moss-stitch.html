[stag_intro]In today's Saturday Stitches, we'll introduce you to the gorgeous texture of moss stitch.[/stag_intro] Here at WATG, we love the simplicity and versatility of this stitch. Work it in Shiny Happy Cotton for something sophisticated and feminine, like our Julia Sweater, or choose the Snood Dogg in our Crazy Sexy Wool, perfect now the nights are drawing in. Moss stitch also looks great paired with ribbing, just check out our Little Giants T-shirt or Riri Shrug if you need convincing!

Here, we'll show you how to work moss stitch over an uneven number of stitches. Click on the image below to watch our video tutorial or read on to get started.

  • 1. Knit 1 stitch.
  • 2. Bring the yarn between the 2 needles to the front.
  • 3. Purl 1 stitch.
  • 4. Bring the yarn between the 2 needles to the back.

Repeat steps 1 to 4 until you have 1 stitch left of your row, knit this stitch. Simply repeat this row to keep knitting in moss stitch.

Want to work moss stitch over an even number of stitches? No problem!

  • ROW 1
  • 1. Knit 1 stitch.
  • 2. Purl 1 stitch.
  • Repeat these 2 steps until you have worked all of the stitches of your row.
  • ROW 2
  • 1. Purl 1 stitch.
  • 2. Knit 1 stitch.
  • Repeat these 2 steps until you have worked all of the stitches of your row.

Keep repeating these 2 rows form the pattern.
