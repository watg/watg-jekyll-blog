[stag_intro]A lot of you have been asking for a DIY post on how to make a pom pom with your leftover yarn. We listened and here it is![/stag_intro]

(Small random fact: Pom Pom is derived from the French word pompon, which refers to a small decorative ball made of fabric.) Be warned this post contains a LOT of fluff, so make sure you have a bin on standby!)

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/1.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

THE TOOLS: Crazy Sexy Wool (leftovers) and scissors!

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/2.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Hold the tail of yarn firmly in your hand under your thumb.

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/3.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Start to wrap (not too tightly!) around your fingers, as neatly as you can.

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/4.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/5.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Wrap around your hand roughly 40 times. The denser you want your Pom Pom the the more wool you will need to wrap around your hand.

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/6.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/7.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Slide off your hand…

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/8.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Snip off an extra bit of wool and place underneath and tie it in a tight knot.

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/9.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/10.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/11.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/12.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Time to get scissor happy! Now you turn it to the side and start cutting through the wool. All the way round!

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/13.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/14.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/15.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Start trimming the Pom Pom so it starts to become even and neat. You can cut it down to whatever size you like.

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/16.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Give it a little love by fluffing it up in your hands ruffling the Pom Pom to see if any sneaky bits of wool got missed.

 [stag_image style="no-filter" src="https://watg-blog.s3.amazonaws.com/2014/05/17.jpg" alignment="center" url=""]

Taaaaa dah! Hello Pom Pom. Now you can do as you please. For a hat or for decoration, go wild!
