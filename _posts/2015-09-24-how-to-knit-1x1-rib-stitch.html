[stag_intro]For today's Saturday Stitches we are showing you how to knit 1x1 rib stitch.[/stag_intro] Ribbing is a super-important component of knitwear. By alternating between knit and purl stitches you create an elastic fabric which is often found on cuffs, necklines and hats. Anywhere that needs to be stretchy, really!

Here, we're showing you how to work in 1x1 rib stitch across an even number of stitches. Since ribbing is stretchy, it's a good idea to use a stretchy cast on, too! We suggest checking out the long-tail cast on technique. Ready to get ribbing? Then click on the image below to watch our video, or read on for our step-by-step guide.

  • 1. Knit 1 stitch.
  • 2. Bring the yarn between the needles to the front.
  • 3. Purl your next stitch.
  • 4. Bring the yarn between the needles to the back.

Repeat steps 1 to 4 until you have worked all of the stitches of your row. ThenÂ simply repeat this row to keep knitting in 1x1 rib stitch.
